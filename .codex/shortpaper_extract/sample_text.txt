Abstract—Effective customer relationshipmanagement (CRM) is a challenge for small carworkshops due to the lack of a dedicated system.This project proposes aportal-basedCRMsystemto improve customer engagement and streamlineoperations. The system includes a user-friendlywebsite with WhatsApp integration for directcommunication, a dashboard for managing clientinformation, booking requests, and services. Keyfeatures include appointment scheduling,feedback collection, and robust securitymeasures.Using an Agile methodology ensures reliabilityand effectiveness. This solution addresses currentchallenges, enhancing customer service for bothworkshop owners and customers. Futureimprovements may include payment gatewayintegration, a feedback system, automatedreminders, andexpandedserviceofferings.

V. SystemArchitectureDesign
The Model-View-Controller (MVC) architectureis a widely used design pattern in softwaredevelopment, providing a systematic andorganized approach to building applications. Inthe context of a Customer RelationshipManagement (CRM) system, the MVCarchitecture applied to enhance maintainability,scalability, and flexibility.In the AZ Autoworkscar workshop system, the Model represents theapplication's data and business logic, includingentities like User, Booking, Notification, andAnnouncement, with various attributes andrelationships managing customer data,appointment information, and service-relateddetails. The View comprises user interfaces,such as the login pagefor admins, bookingformfor customers, announcements page, WhatsAppintegration, and admin dashboard featuringsections for Requests, Appointmentsinformation, Service Records, andAnnouncements. It handles the presentationlayer and user interactions. The Controller actsas anintermediarybetweentheModel andView,managing user input, updating the Model, andcontrolling data flow. It includes theAuthController for login and logout functions,BookingController for handlingbooking-related actions like creating, storing,viewing, approving, declining, updating,deleting, and marking bookings as done,NotificationController for sending email andWhatsApp notifications,AnnouncementController for creating, editing,deleting, and viewing announcements, andServiceRecordController for viewing,searching, and deleting service records. Thiscomprehensive MVC architecture ensures aseamless and efficient workflow, enabling usersto interact with the system effectively whileallowing admins to manage all aspects ofcustomer interactionsandservicerecords.

VI. DatabaseDesign
The database design for the CustomerRelationship Management (CRM) system ismeticulously crafted to efficiently managecustomer-related information, appointments,notifications, and communication logs. Keyentities include "Customers" to store clientdetails,and "Admins"for systemadministrators,"Appointments" for scheduling service sessions,and "Notifications" for sending messages tocustomers. The "WhatsApp Messages" entitycaptures real-timeinteractions. Relationshipsareestablished, linking appointments, notifications,and messages to the respective customers andadministrators. This design ensures dataintegrity, facilitating seamless retrieval andmanipulation of information critical forcustomer engagement and service coordination.The system accommodates diversefunctionalities, enabling effectivecommunication, appointment scheduling, andcustomer relationship management, contributingto a robust and scalable CRM solution.In thecontext of the AZ Autoworks CRM system,various entities play critical roles. The Adminentity represents system administratorsmanaging bookings, appointments, services, andannouncements, with attributes like admin_id,username, password, andemail. TheCustomer
entity represents users booking appointmentsand receiving services, characterized bycustomer_id, username, email, phone number,car brand, andplatenumber. TheBookingentityencapsulates customer service bookings withattributes suchasbooking_id, customer_id, date,time, and status (e.g., pending, approved,declined). The Appointment entity representsscheduled and approved bookings, includingappointment_id, booking_id, status (e.g., ontime, overdue), and is_done to indicatecompletion. The ServiceRecord entity logscompleted services, featuring service_record_id,appointment_id, service_date, and rating (e.g.,average, good, excellent). The Announcemententity contains admin-created announcementsfor customers, with attributes likeannouncement_id, title, content, image_url, andcreated_at. Lastly, the Notification entityrepresents communications sent to customersregarding booking statuses, includingnotification_id, customer_id, message, andsent_at. Each entity'sattributesandrelationshipsensure comprehensive management of the CRMsystem’sfunctionalities.
